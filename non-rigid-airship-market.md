<p><p>A non-rigid airship, also known as a blimp, is a type of airship that has no internal structural framework or keel supporting its outer envelope. Instead, it relies on the pressure of the lifting gas inside the envelope to maintain its shape. Non-rigid airships are typically smaller and less expensive to operate than rigid airships, making them popular for advertising, surveillance, and tourism purposes.</p><p>The non-rigid airship market is expected to experience significant growth with a CAGR of 12.6% during the forecast period. Gas airships, on the other hand, utilize helium or hydrogen gas for lift and propulsion, making them ideal for surveillance, filming, and scientific research.
