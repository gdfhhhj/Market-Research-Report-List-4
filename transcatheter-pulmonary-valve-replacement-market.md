<p><p>Transcatheter Pulmonary Valve Replacement is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat pulmonary valve disease. There are two main types of valves used in this procedure: Balloon-Expanded and Self-Expanded. </p><p>Balloon-Expanded valves are inserted via a catheter and expanded using a balloon once in position, providing controlled deployment. Self-Expanded valves, on the other hand, are made of materials that expand on their own once deployed in the heart. Both types offer advantages in terms of precision and ease of implantation, catering to different patient needs.</p></p>
