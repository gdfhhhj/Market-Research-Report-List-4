<p><p>Peripheral nerve injuries can be treated using different methods, including direct repair, nerve grafting, nerve conduit, and other innovative approaches. Direct repair involves stitching the damaged nerve ends together, while nerve grafting uses a nerve from another part of the body to bridge the gap. Nerve conduit involves placing a tube-like structure to guide nerve regeneration. Other emerging treatments include stem cell therapy and nerve transfer techniques. Each method has its own advantages and is selected based on the type and severity of the nerve injury.</p></p>
