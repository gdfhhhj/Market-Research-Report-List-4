<p><p>Compression bone screws can be classified into three main types based on their design and intended use. Cortical screws are designed for use in denser bone tissue, such as the outer layer of bones. Cancellous screws are meant for softer, more porous bone tissue found in the inner parts of bones. Other types of compression bone screws include specialized screws with unique features for specific surgical procedures. Each type of screw is tailored to provide optimal compression and stability for different types of bone tissue.</p></p>
