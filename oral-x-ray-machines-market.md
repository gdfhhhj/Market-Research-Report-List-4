<p><p>Oral X-Ray machines are essential tools in dental practices for diagnosing and treating oral health issues. There are two main types of Oral X-Ray machines on the market- Analog X-Ray machines and Digital X-Ray machines. </p><p>Analog X-Ray machines use traditional photographic film to capture images, which are then developed and viewed in a darkroom. On the other hand, Digital X-Ray machines use electronic sensors to capture images, which can be viewed instantly on a computer screen. Digital X-Ray machines offer advantages such as faster processing time, lower radiation exposure, and easier storage and sharing of images.</p></p>
