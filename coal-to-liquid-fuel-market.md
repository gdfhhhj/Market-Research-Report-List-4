<p><ul><li>Direct Liquefaction</li><li>Indirect Liquefaction</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Coal to Liquid Fuel (CTL) market involves two main types of processes: direct liquefaction and indirect liquefaction. </p><p>Direct liquefaction is a process where coal is directly converted into liquid fuels through chemical reactions under high temperature and pressure. This method has higher efficiency but requires a more complex infrastructure.</p><p>Indirect liquefaction involves first gasifying coal to produce syngas, which is then converted into liquid fuels through Fischer-Tropsch synthesis. This method is more widely used due to its flexibility in utilizing different feedstocks and producing a variety of fuels.</p></p>
