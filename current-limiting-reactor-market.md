<p><ul><li>Shunt Reactor</li><li>Series Reactor</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Current Limiting Reactors are used in power systems to limit the fault current in the event of a short circuit. Shunt Reactors are connected in parallel to the power system and are designed to absorb reactive power under normal operating conditions. Series Reactors, on the other hand, are connected in series with the power system and are used to limit the current during short circuits. Shunt Reactors help stabilize voltage levels, while Series Reactors help protect equipment and reduce stresses on the power system.</p></p>
