<p><p>Autologous Matrix-induced Chondrogenesis (AMIC) is a surgical procedure that aims to repair damaged cartilage in the knee joint. <p><p>Autologous matrix-induced chondrogenesis market involves the use of various types of matrices to facilitate cartilage regeneration. Hyaluronic acid, collagen, polyethylene glycol (PEG), and poly lactic-co-glycolic acid (PLGA) are common types of matrices used in this process. Hyaluronic acid provides lubrication and shock absorption, collagen offers structural support, PEG enhances cell attachment and proliferation, and PLGA provides biodegradable scaffolding. Each type of matrix plays a crucial role in promoting chondrogenesis and ultimately, cartilage repair.</p></p>
