<p><p>GPS bike computers come in two main market types: mapping and non-mapping. Mapping GPS bike computers provide detailed navigation and mapping features, allowing cyclists to plan and follow routes with turn-by-turn directions. On the other hand, non-mapping GPS bike computers focus more on performance metrics such as speed, distance, heart rate, and power output without detailed mapping capabilities. Both types cater to different needs and preferences of cyclists, with mapping devices appealing to those who prioritize navigation and exploration, while non-mapping devices are preferred by those primarily focused on performance tracking.</p></p>
