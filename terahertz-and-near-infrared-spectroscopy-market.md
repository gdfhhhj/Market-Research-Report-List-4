<p><p>Terahertz and near-infrared spectroscopy markets can be divided into three main types based on the wavelength range used: near-infrared, mid-infrared, and far-infrared. Near-infrared spectroscopy uses wavelengths from 700 nm to 2500 nm, suitable for analyzing organic compounds and pharmaceuticals. Mid-infrared spectroscopy covers the range between 2.5 µm to 25 µm, ideal for studying inorganic materials and chemicals. Far-infrared spectroscopy operates at wavelengths above 25 µm, commonly used in the study of low-frequency vibrations in solid-state materials. Each type of spectroscopy offers unique advantages for various applications.</p></p>
