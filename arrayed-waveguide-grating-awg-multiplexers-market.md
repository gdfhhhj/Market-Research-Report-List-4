<p><p>Arrayed Waveguide Grating (AWG) multiplexers are optical devices used in wavelength division multiplexing (WDM) systems to combine and separate different wavelengths of light. Two main types of AWG multiplexers are Thermal AWG and Athermal AWG. </p><p>Thermal AWG multiplexers require active thermal tuning to maintain peak performance across different temperatures, making them more suitable for specific applications where temperature stability is critical. On the other hand, Athermal AWG multiplexers use passive mechanisms to achieve temperature stability, offering a more cost-effective and reliable solution for most WDM system implementations.</p></p>
