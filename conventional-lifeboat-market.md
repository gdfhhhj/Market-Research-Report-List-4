<p><p>Conventional lifeboats can be divided into two main market types: closed lifeboat and free fall lifeboat. Closed lifeboats are designed with a fully enclosed structure to protect passengers from harsh weather conditions and high waves. They are commonly used on oil rigs and large commercial vessels. On the other hand, free fall lifeboats are equipped with a mechanism that allows them to drop rapidly into the water from a high position, providing a quick escape in emergency situations. Both types of lifeboats offer crucial safety features for maritime operations.</p></p>
