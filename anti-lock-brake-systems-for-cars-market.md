<p><p>Anti-lock Brake Systems (ABS) for cars come in different market types based on the number of channels they use to control braking. One-channel ABS uses one control for both front wheels, while two-channel ABS has separate controls for each front wheel. Three-channel ABS adds a control for the rear wheels, and four-channel ABS has individual controls for all four wheels. These different types of ABS systems help improve braking performance and prevent wheel lock-up in various driving conditions.</p></p>
