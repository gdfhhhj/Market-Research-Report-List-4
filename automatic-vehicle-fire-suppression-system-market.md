<p><p>Automatic Vehicle Fire Suppression Systems can be categorized into two main types: Wet Systems and Dry Systems. Wet Systems use water or other liquid fire suppressants that are stored under pressure, ready to be released when a fire is detected. Dry Systems, on the other hand, utilize powders or gaseous agents as fire suppressants. Both systems are designed to quickly and effectively extinguish fires in vehicles to prevent catastrophic damage and ensure passenger safety. Each system has unique advantages and is chosen based on specific vehicle requirements.</p></p>
