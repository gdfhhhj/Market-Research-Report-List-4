<p><p>Fire-resistant plasterboards are available in two main types: Tapered-Edged and Square-Edged. Tapered-Edged plasterboards have tapered edges, making it easier to create a smooth, seamless finish when joining boards together. This type is typically used in areas where a polished aesthetic is desired. On the other hand, Square-Edged plasterboards have square edges and are commonly used in areas where a more robust finish is needed. Both types offer fire resistance, providing added safety in the event of a fire.</p></p>
