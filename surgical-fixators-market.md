<p><p>Surgical fixators are devices used to stabilize fractured bones during the healing process. Internal fixators such as plates, screws, and nails are placed inside the body to hold the fracture in place. External fixators, on the other hand, are placed outside the body and can be unilateral or bilateral, circular, or hybrid. These devices provide stability and support to allow proper healing of the bone. Both internal and external fixators are essential tools in orthopedic surgery for treating complex fractures.</p></p>
